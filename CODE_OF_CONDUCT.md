# Floatair Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a respectful, professional, and welcoming environment for everyone who participates in Floatair.

This applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, documentation, and any other project space connected to this repository.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive when discussing ideas, code, design, documentation, or product decisions.
- Assume good intent, especially when reviewing work across different disciplines or levels of experience.
- Keep feedback specific, actionable, and focused on the work.
- Help maintain a collaborative environment where people can ask questions and improve the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Repeated disruption of discussions, reviews, or project decisions.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Any behavior that creates an unsafe or hostile project environment.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, commits, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently limit participation for behavior that is inappropriate, harmful, or disruptive to the project.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, contact the project maintainers through the appropriate repository channel. If the report includes private or sensitive information, use a private maintainer contact channel when available.

Reports will be reviewed with care and handled as confidentially as practical.
